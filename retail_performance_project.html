AI-Powered Reporting Workflow

Agent 1: Insight extraction
A CrewAI agent used PDFSearchTool to read the exported dashboard report and extract verified sales, profitability, and discount-impact findings, grounding outputs in the actual analysis rather than generic summaries.
Agent 2: Executive email
A second agent, powered by GPT-4.1, transformed the verified findings into a concise, professional executive summary email and sent it automatically through the Gmail integration.
Sequential pipeline
The two agents ran in a sequential workflow built in CrewAI Studio, demonstrating an end-to-end pipeline from raw analytics output to a delivered business communication with no manual write-up required.

Key Findings

Discounts erode profitability
Higher discount rates were strongly associated with declining profit margins, with several sub-categories turning unprofitable beyond a certain discount threshold.
Sales vs. profit don't align
Some of the highest-selling categories generated the weakest profit, while lower-volume categories delivered stronger margins — highlighting where discounting strategy needs to change.
What-If scenario impact
Adjusting the discount-rate parameter in the What-If scenario showed that trimming discounts on low-margin sub-categories could recover a meaningful share of lost profit without major sales loss.
Forecast trend
The sales forecast highlighted seasonal patterns and projected near-term trends, supporting more informed inventory and promotional planning.
Filter-driven exploration
Cross-chart filter actions let users drill from region and category down to sub-category level, making it easy to isolate where discounting was most damaging to profit.
Automated executive reporting
The CrewAI workflow turned these findings into a ready-to-send executive email, showing how AI agents can close the loop between analysis and business communication.
